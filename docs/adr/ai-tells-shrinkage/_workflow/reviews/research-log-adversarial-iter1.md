### A6 [suggestion]
**Certificate**: Assumption test — removing the curly-quotes and knowledge-cutoff rules forfeits no value the project's own decision test would keep.
**Target**: Decision Log 2026-06-19T09:41:24Z, REMOVE set includes "Curly quotes" and "Knowledge-cutoff disclaimers".
**Challenge**: The decision test is "keep a rule iff it would change prose a human reader also finds unclear, imprecise, or hard to skim." Knowledge-cutoff disclaimers ("As of my knowledge cutoff...") are pure concealment with no reader value, so removing them passes the test cleanly. Curly quotes are borderline under the *stated* test: the curly-quotes rule (`house-style.md:348-349`) is justified by ecosystem consistency with straight-quote tooling ("where the surrounding ecosystem uses straight quotes"), which is a tooling/diff-hygiene rationale, not purely an AI-authorship tell. A reviewer copy-pasting from a word processor produces curly quotes that break grep and code-fence matching, a real friction independent of LLM authorship. Classifying it as "concealment-only" is the weakest member of the REMOVE set.
**Stress scenario**: A human author pastes `"foo"` (curly) into a track file; a later `grep '"foo"'` (straight) misses it. The friction is real and human-caused, which is exactly what the keep-test is supposed to retain.
**Code evidence**: `house-style.md:348-349` states the rationale as ecosystem straight-quote consistency, not as an AI tell. `house-style.md:147` (knowledge-cutoff) is unambiguously concealment-only by contrast.
**Verdict**: HOLDS for knowledge-cutoff; FRAGILE for curly quotes — the survival of the decision is fine (curly quotes are a near-zero-churn rule, so removing it costs little), but the *classification* as concealment-only is contestable under the project's own test.
**Proposed fix**: Either keep the curly-quotes rule (its rationale is ecosystem/tooling, surviving the keep-test), or, if it is removed, record that the removal is on a low-value/low-churn basis rather than the concealment-only basis, so the classification stays honest to the decision test.
