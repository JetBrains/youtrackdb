## Decision Log

#### D1: Remove disguise-only style rules across the whole style machinery (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** The disguise-only rules make text look human-authored while adding no comprehension value; they fight model training (cost quality/effort) and the plan documents remain hard to read despite them. Removal applies at the source (`house-style.md`) so documents and chat both inherit. Mirrored consumers that update in the same change (enumerated by grep over the removed pattern names — "negative parallelism", "Title Case", "hyphenated", "curly", "closing phrases", "roundabout negation" — re-derivable the same way): the regex checker (`design-mechanical-checks.py` dsc-ai-tell: `NEGATIVE_PARALLELISM_RE`, `NEGATIVE_PARALLELISM_TRAILING_RE`, `HYPHENATED_PAIR_CLUSTER_RE`, the Title-Case check), its test suite `.claude/scripts/tests/test_dsc_ai_tell.py` and fixture `.claude/scripts/tests/fixtures/dsc-ai-tell-fixture.md` (pins the regexes — build breaks if regexes go and tests stay), `.claude/skills/ai-tells/SKILL.md` (its **always-loaded** `description:` frontmatter hard-codes negative parallelism, Title Case headings, and closing phrases — three of the six removals — plus body catalogue entries), `review-workflow-writing-style.md`, `house-conversation.md`, `design-document-rules.md:289` (the dsc-ai-tell catalogue row naming negative parallelism, Title Case, hyphenated pairs, and curly quotes among its patterns), and the project `CLAUDE.md` § Writing Style paragraph (its parenthetical names the "It's not X — it's Y" negative-parallelism exemplar) (strengthened per gate A2, completed per gate A10).
- **Alternatives rejected:** Chat-register-only removal — the review cost lives in the durable documents, so keeping disguise rules there keeps fighting training on the surface that matters most.

#### D2: Persona readers return hybrid output where the narrative has a consumer — narrative + findings on the whole-doc gate; persona-voiced findings only on the sliced auditor (2026-07-02T08:35Z, refined 06:40Z per gate A4) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** The narrative preserves the reading-experience signal the book's beta-reader model demonstrated (per-chapter stumbles, painful moments, navigation friction) — but that signal is a whole-read property (the book's beta readers read the full book in order; their best observations span chapters), so the narrative half attaches only to the **whole-doc comprehension/time-constrained reviewer**, whose reading narrative is meaningful and whose consumer is the orchestrator's gate verdict plus the author's rework seed. The **range-sliced readability auditor** returns persona-voiced structured findings only: at ~200-line slice scope a "stumble" is exactly what a finding already records (verbatim quote, location, why-it-breaks), and a per-slice narrative would flow into the orchestrator with no consumer, multiplied by up to ~6 slices × 3 rounds. The structured findings keep the dual-clean convergence and settled-state mechanics keyed on discrete findings in both roles.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Pure narrative + synthesis step (book model verbatim) — larger loop rework, orchestrator must parse prose into revision items. Manifest-only with persona identity — loses the experiential signal on the whole-doc surface where it is real. Uniform hybrid on both roles — the auditor-path narrative has no consumer and only costs context (gate A4).

#### D3: readability-auditor and comprehension-review move to Sonnet; design-author pinned to Opus, never Fable (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** Sonnet as mid-level-reader proxy — if Sonnet can follow the document, a mid-level developer can; the reader roles judge reading experience, not generate designs. The author stays Opus because it drafts from an already-resolved research log: the hard decisions are settled before authoring, so Fable adds nothing there (user-stated clarification). **False-clean risk and mitigation (strengthened per gate A6):** the proxy claim covers only the benign direction; the load-bearing direction is whether a weaker reader *reports* what it failed to follow rather than glossing over it. Three mitigations: (1) the persona contract targets exactly that failure mode — a target reader instructed "you will not fill in gaps" reports failure-to-follow ("I had to re-read this") rather than assessing style, which is introspectively easier than judging subtle prose quality, and the whole-doc gate's output is answers-plus-citations, where failure is *visible* (a wrong or uncited answer) rather than silent; (2) Opus readers carry the opposite error at similar cost — a strong model follows dense prose a mid-level human cannot, silently under-reporting too-dense (the calibration failure this change exists to fix); (3) backstops remain — the dsc-ai-tell regex pre-flight, and the Phase-C `review-workflow-writing-style` pass on workflow-markdown diffs. Residual risk accepted and named: if dual-clean documents keep drawing human readability complaints, the model pin is the first suspect to revisit.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Keep Opus readers — defeats the reader-proxy calibration (opposite-direction false-clean: follows what the target human cannot) and costs more. Keep one Opus backstop on the outer gate only — rejected because the gate's answers-plus-citations output already makes its failure visible, which is the honest mitigation; splitting the pins re-introduces the calibration mismatch on the gate that most needs the proxy. Fable for the author — unnecessary given the resolved-log input.

#### D4: Rename TL;DR → Summary as a `### Summary` sub-heading with details under their own sub-headings; legacy TL;DR spellings stay accepted (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** A sub-heading gives structural (not merely typographic) separation of summary from detail — skimmable end-to-end and regex-enforceable; "Summary" is self-explanatory to readers where "TL;DR" is jargon. Backward compatibility follows the D11 References→Decisions & invariants precedent (both spellings accepted). **Complete site list (strengthened per gate A3, enumerated by grep for `TL;DR` over `.claude/`):** `design-document-rules.md` (per-section shape, Part-level TL;DR, consolidation TL;DR exception, templates); `design-mechanical-checks.py` — the `section_has_tldr` regexes at :708-714, `SHAPE_EXEMPT_SECTION_NAMES` ("TL;DR" Part-level entry), and **`MANDATORY_OR_FORM_SUBHEADINGS` at :63-71** (must gain "summary" alongside "tl;dr", both spellings, or the same-shape sibling similarity check counts the shared `### Summary` heading in every section and false-positives on every well-formed design); `prompts/design-review.md` (structural findings + TOC row); `edit-design/SKILL.md` (~5 sites); `planning.md:774`; `house-style.md` § Navigability; `prompts/create-final-design.md:187`; `skills/review-workflow-pr/SKILL.md:114`; `conventions.md:149`; `agents/comprehension-review.md:34`; the dsc test suite where it pins TL;DR shapes.
- **Alternatives rejected:** `**Summary.**` bold-prefix rename only — the summary still runs into the details, which is the separation failure the user named. Hard rename without legacy acceptance — breaks every committed legacy design under `docs/adr/**` on re-review.

#### D5: Technical-writer voice governs design docs and track files; issue/PR/commit surfaces keep the terse BLUF register (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe] (user-defaulted: stated as assumption, not overridden)
- **Why:** The teaching-narrative voice suits documents read to build a mental model; issues and PR bodies are read to act, where brevity wins. A narrative arc in an issue body adds length without comprehension gain. **Track-file anchor carve (strengthened per gate A7, mirroring the D8 design-doc guard):** in track files the voice governs the prose sections only — `## Purpose / Big Picture`, `## Context and Orientation`, `## Plan of Work` — while the structured ExecPlan surfaces stay registry-terse: the `## Concrete Steps` roster lines, `## Episodes` structured-field blocks, `## Decision Log` four-bullet records, `## Invariants & Constraints` bullets, and `## Interfaces and Dependencies` boundary lists. Track files are read by implementer spawns under context budget; narrativizing the structured fields would inflate every implementer read with no reader who benefits. The prose trio is exhaustive (closure per gate A12): every other track-file section — including the author-written `## Validation and Acceptance` and `## Idempotence and Recovery` — stays registry-terse.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Voice everywhere — worsens the act-oriented surfaces. Voice over whole track files without the carve — an author agent could legitimately narrativize rosters and episode fields (gate A7's scenario).

#### D6: Persona roster — recast the two existing cold readers; no new spawns; mechanical checks stay personless (2026-07-02T08:50Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved. readability-auditor → *target reader* (mid-level dev assigned to implement/review, "will not fill in gaps", persona-voiced structured findings per refined D2). comprehension-review → *time-constrained reviewer* (thirty minutes to build a working mental model; owns navigability/dip-in checks it already has; hybrid narrative + findings per refined D2); the skeptical veteran's depth-allergy folds into its mental-model verdict rather than a third spawn. absorption-check / fidelity-check remain personless coverage cross-checks. **Axis ownership survives the recast (strengthened per gate A1):** the persona governs register and stance, not the checklist — the target reader **keeps sole ownership of the prose AI-tell judgment axis** (the S4 one-owner-per-surface invariant), and its findings retain the house-style `§`-citation obligation for the kept judgment rules (padding, hedging, elegant variation, boldface cap — the subset the dsc regexes cannot reach). A stumble report and a `§`-cited finding are the same artifact viewed from the persona: "I had to re-read this" + quote + the rule it breaks.
- **Alternatives rejected:** A third veteran spawn — extra cost per round for a signal the comprehension verdict can carry. New persona agents alongside the old roles — duplicates the review surface. Persona-without-checklist (pure experiential reader) — silently drops the judgment half of the axis and leaves the kept D7 rules with no per-round enforcer (gate A1's scenario).

#### D7: Per-rule disposition for the disguise-only removal (2026-07-02T08:50Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved the split. **Remove** from `house-style.md` (and mirrored consumers): negative-parallelism ban, roundabout-negation ban, closing-phrases ban, hyphenated-word-pair rule, curly-quotes rule, sentence-case heading mandate (Title Case allowed again). **Keep:** Orientation + Plain language wholesale; passive voice, nominalization, broken-grammar-around-identifiers; elegant variation; boldface cap; throat-clearing, prompt-restating, superficial -ing (padding = review cost); hedge stacking, filler hedges, **trailing hedges**, vague attribution, false ranges, generic positive conclusions, **persuasive authority tropes** (substance/precision — all six force a claim to carry its number, source, or named trade-off, or perform depth without content; orthogonal to disguise); mechanism traces; BLUF rules hardened per YTDB-1163 alongside the D4 `### Summary` shape. **Closure default (strengthened per gate A8): any rule not named in the remove list keeps its current disposition.** The consumer-only "adjective triads" rule (`review-workflow-writing-style.md`, the `ai-tells` catalogue; example "comprehensive, robust, scalable") is a **distinct vague-adjective-stack rule, not** the hyphenated-pair rule's echo (corrected per gate A11): it lands in **keep** under this decision's own criterion — it forces substance (each vague adjective must become a named quality, the § Plain language "name the quality" move) — and stays at its consumer sites; it gains no new house-style section in this change. **Why negative parallelism is remove while throat-clearing is keep (the distinguishing argument gate A8 demanded):** throat-clearing is additive filler — deleting it shortens the text with zero information loss, so its ban pays in review time. Negative parallelism *reframes* real content at near-zero length cost — "not X but Y" carries the X-contrast a positive rewrite often has to re-state — so its ban buys no brevity, only disguise, and its enforcement is the most false-positive-prone regex in the checker (the trailing-negation variant needed a curated intensifier discriminator to stop flagging legitimate contrasts). Removal criterion = disguise-only benefit + enforcement cost; keep criterion = deletes text or forces substance.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Keeping the heading-case rule (marginal nav value; textbook fighting-training-for-cosmetics — flagged to user, not pulled back). Removing padding rules as "tells" (they cut review time, so they contribute to the aim).

#### D8: Dual-register design document — frozen `design.md` stays the track-derivation seed; machine-facing skeleton exempt from the narrative voice (2026-07-02T09:05Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User raised whether the narrative format degrades the design as a machine seed for track generation and whether the research log should seed tracks instead. Resolution: the log is a decision ledger (append-only, superseded entries, no integrated mechanism) and cannot supply the mechanism content tracks need; the frozen design is reviewed, integrated, and user-accepted. The seed consumer is itself a cold spawn, which narrative orientation helps. Guard: D-records keep the canonical four-bullet registry-terse form, `### Summary` blocks stay plain-claim and self-contained (YTDB-1163 rule doing double duty), Decisions & invariants footers stay lists, diagrams stay — the technical-writer voice governs only the mechanism prose between these anchors (extends the existing registry-terse vs orientation-register distinction to the design doc's internal structure). `### Summary` separation improves partial-read extractability for derivation spawns.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Research log as the `full`-tier track seed — forces mechanism re-derivation from code, risks divergence from the frozen reviewed design, and loses the integration pass. Applying the narrative voice uniformly to the whole doc including records/summaries — dissolves operative content into the story arc, the extraction hazard the user's doubt named.

#### D9: Book voice-rule transfer boundary (2026-07-02T09:05Z, precedence added 06:40Z per gate A5/A9) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved. Transfer verbatim: concrete-before-abstract, one-concept-per-section, earn-every-name (role first, class name after), diagrams-must-teach, no-bullet-fact-dumps. Transfer adapted: narrative-not-reference at *section* scope (each section one complete argument: problem → forces → mechanism → consequences; document stays dip-in navigable), connect-forward-and-backward as light prose links (opener names what the section builds on, closer names what depends on it). Already present: precise `file:line` citations. **Precedence over kept house-style rules (per gate A5):** (1) in mechanism-overview prose the narrative rules win over the length heuristics — a worked-example opener is a new template-bound exempt category under the section-length cap's exemption list, so the 200-word trigger and the "less text" bias do not flag what the target reader demanded; the anti-padding criterion still applies *inside* the example (a worked example that teaches nothing is padding). (2) Same-shape sibling consolidation yields to one-concept-per-section when the siblings teach distinct concepts; consolidation stays for same-shape *reference* material (tables, catalogues) where no concept is being taught. Any enforcement agent reads the ranked rule, so the author and the style reviewer cannot hold contradictory targets (the loop-thrash scenario). **Link-staleness hardening (per gate A9, suggestion recorded):** the adapted forward/backward links cite the neighbor's section heading verbatim (greppable), and the `edit-design` mutation discipline gains a reminder to re-read neighbor openers/closers on section-move/section-remove/section-rename.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Whole-document story arc (design docs are consulted, not read cover-to-cover); dropping forward/backward links entirely (the dip-in personas in the book's beta feedback showed they carry navigation); leaving the two rule sets unranked — two enforcement agents with contradictory targets thrash the authoring loop (gate A5's scenario).
