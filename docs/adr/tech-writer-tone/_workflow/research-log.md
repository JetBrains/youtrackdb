## Decision Log

#### D1: Remove disguise-only style rules across the whole style machinery (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** The disguise-only rules make text look human-authored while adding no comprehension value; they fight model training (cost quality/effort) and the plan documents remain hard to read despite them. Removal applies at the source (`house-style.md`) so documents and chat both inherit; the regex checker (`design-mechanical-checks.py` dsc-ai-tell), `review-workflow-writing-style.md`, and `house-conversation.md` update to match.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Chat-register-only removal — the review cost lives in the durable documents, so keeping disguise rules there keeps fighting training on the surface that matters most.

#### D2: Persona readers return hybrid output — first-person narrative reading report plus a structured findings list (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** The narrative preserves the reading-experience signal the book's beta-reader model demonstrated (per-chapter stumbles, painful moments); the structured findings appendix keeps the authoring loop's dual-clean convergence and settled-state mechanics keyed on discrete findings.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Pure narrative + synthesis step (book model verbatim) — larger loop rework, orchestrator must parse prose into revision items. Manifest-only with persona identity — loses the experiential signal that made the book reviews effective.

#### D3: readability-auditor and comprehension-review move to Sonnet; design-author pinned to Opus, never Fable (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** Sonnet as mid-level-reader proxy — if Sonnet can follow the document, a mid-level developer can; the reader roles judge reading experience, not generate designs. The author stays Opus because it drafts from an already-resolved research log: the hard decisions are settled before authoring, so Fable adds nothing there (user-stated clarification).
- **Alternatives rejected:** Keep Opus readers — defeats the reader-proxy calibration and costs more. Fable for the author — unnecessary given the resolved-log input.

#### D4: Rename TL;DR → Summary as a `### Summary` sub-heading with details under their own sub-headings; legacy TL;DR spellings stay accepted (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** A sub-heading gives structural (not merely typographic) separation of summary from detail — skimmable end-to-end and regex-enforceable; "Summary" is self-explanatory to readers where "TL;DR" is jargon. Backward compatibility follows the D11 References→Decisions & invariants precedent (both spellings accepted).
- **Alternatives rejected:** `**Summary.**` bold-prefix rename only — the summary still runs into the details, which is the separation failure the user named.

#### D5: Technical-writer voice governs design docs and track files; issue/PR/commit surfaces keep the terse BLUF register (2026-07-02T08:35Z) [ctx=safe] (user-defaulted: stated as assumption, not overridden)
- **Why:** The teaching-narrative voice suits documents read to build a mental model; issues and PR bodies are read to act, where brevity wins. A narrative arc in an issue body adds length without comprehension gain.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Voice everywhere — worsens the act-oriented surfaces.

#### D6: Persona roster — recast the two existing cold readers; no new spawns; mechanical checks stay personless (2026-07-02T08:50Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved. readability-auditor → *target reader* (mid-level dev assigned to implement/review, "will not fill in gaps", first-person slice report + structured findings appendix per D2). comprehension-review → *time-constrained reviewer* (thirty minutes to build a working mental model; owns navigability/dip-in checks it already has); the skeptical veteran's depth-allergy folds into its mental-model verdict rather than a third spawn. absorption-check / fidelity-check remain personless coverage cross-checks.
- **Alternatives rejected:** A third veteran spawn — extra cost per round for a signal the comprehension verdict can carry. New persona agents alongside the old roles — duplicates the review surface.

#### D7: Per-rule disposition for the disguise-only removal (2026-07-02T08:50Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved the split. **Remove** from `house-style.md` (and mirrored consumers): negative-parallelism ban, roundabout-negation ban, closing-phrases ban, hyphenated-word-pair rule, curly-quotes rule, sentence-case heading mandate (Title Case allowed again). **Keep:** Orientation + Plain language wholesale; passive voice, nominalization, broken-grammar-around-identifiers; elegant variation; boldface cap; throat-clearing, prompt-restating, superficial -ing (padding = review cost); hedge stacking, filler hedges, vague attribution, false ranges, generic positive conclusions (substance/precision); mechanism traces; BLUF rules hardened per YTDB-1163 alongside the D4 `### Summary` shape.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Keeping the heading-case rule (marginal nav value; textbook fighting-training-for-cosmetics — flagged to user, not pulled back). Removing padding rules as "tells" (they cut review time, so they contribute to the aim).

#### D8: Dual-register design document — frozen `design.md` stays the track-derivation seed; machine-facing skeleton exempt from the narrative voice (2026-07-02T09:05Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User raised whether the narrative format degrades the design as a machine seed for track generation and whether the research log should seed tracks instead. Resolution: the log is a decision ledger (append-only, superseded entries, no integrated mechanism) and cannot supply the mechanism content tracks need; the frozen design is reviewed, integrated, and user-accepted. The seed consumer is itself a cold spawn, which narrative orientation helps. Guard: D-records keep the canonical four-bullet registry-terse form, `### Summary` blocks stay plain-claim and self-contained (YTDB-1163 rule doing double duty), Decisions & invariants footers stay lists, diagrams stay — the technical-writer voice governs only the mechanism prose between these anchors (extends the existing registry-terse vs orientation-register distinction to the design doc's internal structure). `### Summary` separation improves partial-read extractability for derivation spawns.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Research log as the `full`-tier track seed — forces mechanism re-derivation from code, risks divergence from the frozen reviewed design, and loses the integration pass. Applying the narrative voice uniformly to the whole doc including records/summaries — dissolves operative content into the story arc, the extraction hazard the user's doubt named.

#### D9: Book voice-rule transfer boundary (2026-07-02T09:05Z) [ctx=safe]
- **Why:** User approved. Transfer verbatim: concrete-before-abstract, one-concept-per-section, earn-every-name (role first, class name after), diagrams-must-teach, no-bullet-fact-dumps. Transfer adapted: narrative-not-reference at *section* scope (each section one complete argument: problem → forces → mechanism → consequences; document stays dip-in navigable), connect-forward-and-backward as light prose links (opener names what the section builds on, closer names what depends on it). Already present: precise `file:line` citations.
- **Alternatives rejected:** Whole-document story arc (design docs are consulted, not read cover-to-cover); dropping forward/backward links entirely (the dip-in personas in the book's beta feedback showed they carry navigation).

## Surprises & Discoveries

- 2026-07-02T08:05Z [ctx=safe] **YTDB-1163 content.** The section-level BLUF rule licenses opaque forward-reference leads ("This section establishes the three-tier `mutation_kind` dispatch") and bodies that read only as the lead's continuation. Two proposed rules: (1) lead must be a self-contained plain claim (parseable before reading the body; gloss the term in the lead or fall back to why-before-what), (2) body must stand as complete prose with the lead deleted (anaphor opener "This/It/Such" resolving into the lead = finding). Acceptance names four sites: `house-style.md` § BLUF lead / § Orientation, `review-workflow-writing-style.md` BLUF criteria, self-check #9, and an exemplar pair beside `house-style.md:70-74`.
